Jobs and workforce

American Job Center guide for East Tennessee veterans.

Start with official Tennessee workforce pages for jobs, training, resume help and American Job Center routing.

Back to East Tennessee resources
Fast answer: Tennessee says all 95 counties are served by an American Job Center. Use the official finder and the Tennessee veterans program page before you drive.

Who this page is for

Veterans, transitioning service members, spouses and caregivers looking for resume help, job search support, training, certifications, apprenticeships or workforce referrals.

Official starting point

Find a Tennessee American Job CenterOfficial Tennessee workforce finder.TN Virtual American Job Center veterans programOfficial veteran workforce program page.Jobs4TNOfficial Tennessee jobs and workforce portal.

What this office or resource may help with

American Job Centers may help with job search, resumes, workshops, labor market information, training referrals, job placement and Jobs4TN support. Ask directly about veteran services and eligibility.

What to check before you call or drive

Check the official page before you call or drive. Hours, appointment rules and locations can change. Ask whether the center serves your county and whether veteran staff are available.

Before you call or drive

  • Check the official page.
  • Confirm hours.
  • Confirm appointment rules.
  • Ask what documents to bring.
  • Ask whether copies are accepted.
  • Ask whether the office handles your exact issue.
  • Do not send private records to Vols4Vets.

What to bring

Resume, work history, military job history, certificates, training records, education history, job targets and any deadline for unemployment, training or application steps.

What not to send to Vols4Vets

Do not send Social Security numbers, passwords, identity documents or private case details to Vols4Vets.

What happens after this link

  • You may leave Vols4Vets.
  • The official site may ask for private information.
  • Only enter private records on official secure sites or with verified accredited help.
  • Write down confirmation numbers.
  • Save copies of what you submit.
  • Come back to Vols4Vets if you need the next starting point.

Common mistakes

  • Assuming every workforce office has the same veteran staff on the same days.
  • Bringing only military acronyms instead of plain civilian job descriptions.
  • Believing any site can promise a job, interview or hiring outcome.
