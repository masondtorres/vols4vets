Caregiver toolkit

Family caregiver support checklist.

Vols4Vets is not a medical provider. Use official family and caregiver resources.

Back to all toolkits
Fast answer: Start with Military OneSource and VA caregiver support. Do not send medical records to Vols4Vets.

Who this is for

Spouses, parents, adult children, friends and caregivers helping a veteran or service member.

First three steps

  1. Write down who needs help and what kind of support is needed.
  2. Use official family or caregiver resources before sharing private details.
  3. Use official medical or crisis channels for health or safety issues.

What to gather

Relationship to the veteran, county, safe contact method, appointment or deadline information and whether VA care or benefits are involved.

What not to send to Vols4Vets

Do not send Social Security numbers, claim numbers, DD214s, medical records, legal papers, passwords or private case details to Vols4Vets.

Official starting points

Military OneSourceOfficial military family support starting point.VA Caregiver SupportOfficial VA caregiver support.VA family member benefitsOfficial VA family member benefits information.

What happens after this link

  • You may leave Vols4Vets.
  • The official site may ask for private information.
  • Only enter private records on official secure sites or with verified accredited help.
  • Write down confirmation numbers.
  • Save copies of what you submit.
  • Come back to Vols4Vets if you need the next starting point.

Common mistakes

  • Trying to solve a medical issue through a non-medical website.
  • Sending medical records before an official office asks.
  • Assuming caregiver programs have the same eligibility rules for every family.
