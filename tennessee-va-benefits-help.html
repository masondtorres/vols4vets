Tennessee benefits

VA benefits help in Tennessee.

Start with official sources: VA.gov, Tennessee Department of Veterans Services, county veteran service offices and accredited representatives.

Fast answer: Use VA.gov for federal benefits, Tennessee Department of Veterans Services for state programs, and a county veteran service office or accredited representative for claim help.

First three steps

  1. Decide whether the issue is federal VA benefits, Tennessee state benefits or local county help.
  2. Gather basic records and questions before calling.
  3. Use official offices or accredited representatives for claim-specific help.

What to bring

DD214 if available, photo ID, VA letters, marriage or dependent records if relevant, direct deposit details only through official portals, and a list of questions.

Common mistakes

  • Mixing up Tennessee state benefits with federal VA benefits.
  • Paying for help before checking official free options.
  • Sharing claim numbers with an unverified person or website.

Related Vols4Vets pages

How to start a VA disability claim, what to bring to a VSO, replace a DD214.

Safety and privacy note

Vols4Vets is not the VA and is not an accredited claims representative. We do not prepare, file, appeal or manage VA claims. Do not send Social Security numbers, claim numbers or private records to Vols4Vets.
