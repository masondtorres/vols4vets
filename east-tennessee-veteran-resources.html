East Tennessee

Veteran resources in East Tennessee.

A practical routing page for veterans, service members, families and caregivers who need a clear first step.

Fast answer: Start with the official office that owns the problem: VA.gov for federal VA benefits, VA Find Locations for care, Tennessee Department of Veterans Services for state benefits, American Job Centers for work, Legal Aid of East Tennessee for civil legal help, and VA homeless support for housing crisis.

Who this page is for

Use this page if you are in East Tennessee and are not sure whether to start with VA, a county office, state veterans services, workforce help, legal aid or a local support office.

First three steps

  1. Name the problem in one sentence: benefits, health care, housing, work, records, family support or legal help.
  2. Pick the official starting point below and check that office page before you drive or call.
  3. Write down what you need, what county you live in, and any deadline coming up.

Official starting points

VA.govFederal benefits, records, health care and account access.VA Find LocationsVA hospitals, clinics, Vet Centers and offices.Tennessee Department of Veterans ServicesState veteran benefits and official Tennessee routing.Tennessee veteran workforce helpJobs, resumes, training information and American Job Center routing.

Before you call or drive

  • Check the official page.
  • Confirm hours.
  • Confirm appointment rules.
  • Ask what documents to bring.
  • Ask whether copies are accepted.
  • Ask whether the office handles your exact issue.
  • Do not send private records to Vols4Vets.

What to bring

Photo ID, DD214 if available, VA letters, current address, phone number, housing or court notices if relevant, resume for job help and a short written list of questions.

What not to send to Vols4Vets

Do not send Social Security numbers, claim numbers, DD214s, medical records, legal papers, passwords or private case details. Use official portals or the office that asks for the record.

What happens after this link

  • You may leave Vols4Vets.
  • The official site may ask for private information.
  • Only enter private records on official secure sites or with verified accredited help.
  • Write down confirmation numbers.
  • Save copies of what you submit.
  • Come back to Vols4Vets if you need the next starting point.

Common mistakes

  • Starting with a paid service before checking official free options.
  • Driving to an office without confirming hours or appointment rules.
  • Sending private records through normal email.

When to use urgent or crisis help

If there is immediate danger, call 911. For veteran crisis support, call 988 then press 1 or text 838255. If housing is unstable now, start with VA homeless support.
