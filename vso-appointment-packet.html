VSO toolkit

VSO appointment packet.

Use accredited representatives for claim-specific help. Vols4Vets is not accredited.

Back to all toolkits
Fast answer: Bring records to the official office, not to Vols4Vets. Confirm appointment rules before you drive.

Who this is for

Veterans, survivors and family members preparing for a county office, VSO or accredited representative meeting.

First three steps

  1. Confirm the office handles your issue.
  2. Ask whether an appointment is required and what copies to bring.
  3. Write one paragraph explaining the issue and deadline.

What to gather

Photo ID, DD214 if available, VA letters, decision letters, dependent records if relevant, and records the official office asks for.

What not to send to Vols4Vets

Do not send Social Security numbers, claim numbers, DD214s, medical records, legal papers, passwords or private case details to Vols4Vets.

What happens after this link

  • You may leave Vols4Vets.
  • The official site may ask for private information.
  • Only enter private records on official secure sites or with verified accredited help.
  • Write down confirmation numbers.
  • Save copies of what you submit.
  • Come back to Vols4Vets if you need the next starting point.

Common mistakes

  • Showing up without checking appointment rules.
  • Bringing originals but no copies.
  • Expecting Vols4Vets to review or prepare claim records.
