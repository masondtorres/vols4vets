Knox County

Veteran resources for Knox County.

A county-focused starting point for official resources in Knoxville and Knox County.

Fast answer: Use official Knox County, Tennessee, VA and workforce pages to confirm where to start. Check the official office page before you drive or call.

Who this page is for

Knox County veterans, families and caregivers who need local routing for benefits, records, care, work, housing risk or civil legal issues.

First three steps

  1. Confirm your county and the type of help needed.
  2. Start with the official state, VA, workforce or legal aid page that matches the problem.
  3. Ask what documents to bring and whether an appointment is required.

Official starting points

Tennessee Department of Veterans ServicesState veterans services and county routing.Find a VA accredited representative or VSOOfficial VA search for accredited representatives.Find a Tennessee American Job CenterOfficial job center routing.

Before you call or drive

  • Check the official page.
  • Confirm hours.
  • Confirm appointment rules.
  • Ask what documents to bring.
  • Ask whether copies are accepted.
  • Ask whether the office handles your exact issue.
  • Do not send private records to Vols4Vets.

What to bring

Photo ID, DD214 if available, proof of Knox County address if requested, VA letters, resume for job help and deadline notices.

What not to send to Vols4Vets

Do not send Social Security numbers, claim numbers, DD214s, medical records, legal papers, passwords or private case details.

Common mistakes

  • Using the wrong county office for residency-based help.
  • Assuming a private claims business is VA accredited.
  • Waiting to ask for help until a court or benefits deadline is near.

When to use urgent or crisis help

Use 911 for immediate danger. Use 988 then press 1 or text 838255 for veteran crisis support. Use VA homeless support if housing may be lost soon.
