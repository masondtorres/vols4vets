Legal toolkit

Legal aid deadline checklist.

Vols4Vets is not a law firm. This is not legal advice.

Back to all toolkits
Fast answer: Do not wait until the court date. Use official legal aid or licensed attorney help for legal questions.

Who this is for

Veterans and families with civil legal deadlines, court dates, eviction papers or official notices.

First three steps

  1. Write down every deadline and court date.
  2. Use official legal aid or licensed attorney help.
  3. Ask what documents to bring before sending anything.

What to gather

County, court date, notice date, agency letters, lease or contract if relevant, and a short timeline.

What not to send to Vols4Vets

Do not send Social Security numbers, claim numbers, DD214s, medical records, legal papers, passwords or private case details to Vols4Vets.

What happens after this link

  • You may leave Vols4Vets.
  • The official site may ask for private information.
  • Only enter private records on official secure sites or with verified accredited help.
  • Write down confirmation numbers.
  • Save copies of what you submit.
  • Come back to Vols4Vets if you need the next starting point.

Common mistakes

  • Waiting until the hearing date.
  • Assuming legal aid handles every case.
  • Sending legal papers to Vols4Vets.
