Women veterans toolkit

Women veterans official support checklist.

Use official VA women veterans resources and keep private details inside official channels.

Fast answer: Start with VA women veterans resources or the Women Veterans Call Center. Do not send private medical or trauma details to Vols4Vets.

Who this is for

Women veterans and helpers looking for official health care, benefits routing, MST support or privacy-first starting points.

First three steps

  1. Decide whether the need is health care, benefits, crisis support or records.
  2. Use an official VA women veterans resource first.
  3. Keep medical and trauma details inside official VA, medical or crisis channels.

What to gather

County, safe contact method, appointment needs, whether VA care is already set up and questions to ask.

What not to send to Vols4Vets

Do not send Social Security numbers, claim numbers, DD214s, medical records, legal papers, passwords or private case details to Vols4Vets.

Official starting points

VA Women Veterans Health CareOfficial VA women veterans health care information.Women Veterans Call CenterOfficial VA call, text and chat routing.VA MST supportOfficial VA MST support information.

What happens after this link

  • You may leave Vols4Vets.
  • The official site may ask for private information.
  • Only enter private records on official secure sites or with verified accredited help.
  • Write down confirmation numbers.
  • Save copies of what you submit.
  • Come back to Vols4Vets if you need the next starting point.

Common mistakes

  • Sharing sensitive details with a non-medical website.
  • Assuming a general office is the right contact.
  • Waiting to ask for help because records are not organized.
