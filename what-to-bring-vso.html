Appointment prep

What to bring to a Veteran Service Officer.

A simple prep list for a county veteran service office, Veterans Service Organization or accredited representative meeting.

Fast answer: Bring ID, DD214 if available, VA letters, records you already have, and a written list of questions. Ask the office what else is required before the appointment.

First three steps

  1. Call ahead and ask whether the office handles your issue and whether an appointment is required.
  2. Put documents in date order and make copies when possible.
  3. Write down what you want help with in one or two sentences.

What to bring

Photo ID, DD214 if available, VA decision letters, service records, medical records only if requested by the official or accredited helper, marriage or dependent records if relevant, direct deposit information only through official channels, and notes about deadlines.

Common mistakes

  • Showing up without an appointment when one is required.
  • Bringing only originals with no copies.
  • Expecting Vols4Vets to review private claim records.

Safety and privacy note

Vols4Vets is not an accredited claims representative. Do not send Social Security numbers, claim numbers, DD214s, medical records, legal papers, passwords or private case details to Vols4Vets.
