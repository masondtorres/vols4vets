Appointment prep

What to bring to a Veteran Service Officer.

A short checklist for a county veteran service office, VSO or VA accredited representative meeting.

Fast answer: Bring ID, DD214 if available, VA letters, records you already have and a written list of questions. Ask the office what else is required before the appointment.

Who this page is for

Veterans, survivors and family members preparing to meet with a county office, VSO or accredited representative about VA benefits.

First three steps

  1. Call ahead and ask whether the office handles your issue.
  2. Ask whether an appointment is required and what records to bring.
  3. Write one paragraph explaining what you need help with.

Official starting points

Find a VA accredited representative or VSOOfficial VA search for accredited help.Tennessee Department of Veterans ServicesState and county veterans services routing.VA accredited representative FAQsLearn what accreditation means.

Before you call or drive

  • Check the official page.
  • Confirm hours.
  • Confirm appointment rules.
  • Ask what documents to bring.
  • Ask whether copies are accepted.
  • Ask whether the office handles your exact issue.
  • Do not send private records to Vols4Vets.

What to bring

Photo ID, DD214 if available, VA decision letters, service records, medical records only if requested by the official or accredited helper, marriage or dependent records if relevant and notes about deadlines.

What not to send to Vols4Vets

Do not send private claim evidence, Social Security numbers, claim numbers, DD214s, medical records, legal papers or passwords.

Common mistakes

  • Showing up without an appointment when one is required.
  • Bringing originals but no copies.
  • Expecting Vols4Vets to review or prepare claim records.

When to use urgent or crisis help

A VSO appointment is not emergency help. Use 911 for immediate danger and 988 then press 1 or text 838255 for veteran crisis support.
