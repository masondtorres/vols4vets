Civil legal help

Veteran legal aid in East Tennessee.

Use this page to find a civil legal help starting point. Vols4Vets is not a law firm and does not give legal advice.

Fast answer: Legal Aid of East Tennessee is a possible civil legal help starting point for eligible residents. Check its official site before you call, drive or rely on service details.

Who this page is for

Veterans and family members with civil legal questions such as housing, benefits paperwork, family issues or consumer problems. These are examples, not promises of service.

First three steps

  1. Write down the legal issue, deadline, court date or notice date.
  2. Check the official legal aid site and eligibility rules.
  3. Use a licensed attorney or legal aid provider for legal advice.

Official starting points

Legal Aid of East TennesseeCivil legal help starting point for eligible East Tennessee residents.Tennessee Free Legal Answers other helpStatewide legal help directory starting point.VA accredited representative searchFor VA claim representation questions, use accredited help.

What to prepare before calling

County, court date or deadline, copies of notices, lease or letters if relevant, names of agencies involved and a short timeline. Do not send these to Vols4Vets.

Common mistakes

  • Waiting until the court date or deadline.
  • Asking Vols4Vets for legal advice.
  • Sending legal papers through normal email before an official provider asks for them.

Safety and privacy note

Vols4Vets is not a law firm and does not provide legal advice. Do not send legal papers, Social Security numbers, claim numbers, medical records, passwords or private case details to Vols4Vets.
