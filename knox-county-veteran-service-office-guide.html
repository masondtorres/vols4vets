Knox County VSO

Knox County Veteran Service Office guide.

Start with official lookup tools and confirm current office details before sharing private records or driving to an office.

Back to East Tennessee resources
Fast answer: Use the TDVS county office lookup and VA accredited representative search first. Vols4Vets is not a claims representative and does not verify or manage claim records.

Who this page is for

Knox County veterans, survivors, family members and caregivers trying to find benefit paperwork help, a county veteran service office, TDVS field office or accredited representative.

Official starting point

TDVS county office lookupOfficial Tennessee lookup for county veterans services offices.TDVS state veterans services officesOfficial TDVS field office page, including Knoxville field office details.VA accredited representative searchOfficial VA tool to check accreditation.

What this office or resource may help with

A county veteran service office or accredited representative may help explain benefit paperwork, prepare applications or identify official next steps. Confirm services, eligibility and appointment rules directly with the office.

What to check before you call or drive

Check the official office page before you call or drive. Hours, appointment rules and locations can change. This page intentionally does not list Knox County phone, address or hours because the official lookup needs manual confirmation before publishing exact details here.

Before you call or drive

  • Check the official page.
  • Confirm hours.
  • Confirm appointment rules.
  • Ask what documents to bring.
  • Ask whether copies are accepted.
  • Ask whether the office handles your exact issue.
  • Do not send private records to Vols4Vets.

What to bring

Photo ID, DD214 if available, VA letters, prior decisions, records the office specifically asks for, and a one-page list of questions and deadlines.

What not to send to Vols4Vets

Do not send Social Security numbers, claim numbers, DD214s, medical records, legal papers, passwords or private case details to Vols4Vets.

What happens after this link

  • You may leave Vols4Vets.
  • The official site may ask for private information.
  • Only enter private records on official secure sites or with verified accredited help.
  • Write down confirmation numbers.
  • Save copies of what you submit.
  • Come back to Vols4Vets if you need the next starting point.

Common mistakes

  • Using an old phone number from a directory instead of an official lookup.
  • Assuming every office handles appeals, benefits, records and state benefits the same way.
  • Sharing claim details before checking accreditation or office identity.

Verification note
