Civil legal help

Legal Aid of East Tennessee veterans guide.

Use Legal Aid of East Tennessee's own website as the starting point for civil legal help questions. Vols4Vets does not give legal advice.

Back to East Tennessee resources
Fast answer: Start with the Legal Aid of East Tennessee official site. It describes civil legal representation across 26 counties and lists issue areas such as housing, family law, domestic violence, elder law and financial protection.

Who this page is for

East Tennessee veterans and families with civil legal questions such as housing, consumer, family, elder law or benefits-adjacent paperwork concerns.

Official starting point

Legal Aid of East TennesseeOfficial LAET website.LAET eventsOfficial event and clinic listings when available.VA accredited representative searchUse for VA claim representation questions, not civil legal advice.

What this office or resource may help with

LAET may help eligible people with civil legal issues. Eligibility, service area, clinics, intake rules and issue coverage must be confirmed directly with LAET.

What to check before you call or drive

Check the official page before you call or drive. Hours, appointment rules, clinic availability and locations can change. Ask whether LAET handles your exact issue and county.

Before you call or drive

  • Check the official page.
  • Confirm hours.
  • Confirm appointment rules.
  • Ask what documents to bring.
  • Ask whether copies are accepted.
  • Ask whether the office handles your exact issue.
  • Do not send private records to Vols4Vets.

What to bring

Photo ID, safe contact method, county, court dates, notices, leases or letters if LAET asks for them, and a short timeline of the issue.

What not to send to Vols4Vets

Do not send legal papers, medical records, Social Security numbers, claim numbers, passwords or private case details to Vols4Vets.

What happens after this link

  • You may leave Vols4Vets.
  • The official site may ask for private information.
  • Only enter private records on official secure sites or with verified accredited help.
  • Write down confirmation numbers.
  • Save copies of what you submit.
  • Come back to Vols4Vets if you need the next starting point.

Common mistakes

  • Waiting until the court date or deadline to ask for help.
  • Assuming legal aid handles every type of case.
  • Sending private legal papers to Vols4Vets instead of using the official intake path.
